Admission Process

The admission process for the MBBS program typically involves the following steps:

  • Initial Inquiry
  • Application Submission
  • Entrance Assessment & Interview
  • Personal Interview
  • Career Pathway Consultation
  • Admission Offer
  • Financial Guidance
  • Enrollment and Orientation

Overview

The MBBS (Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery) is a professional undergraduate medical degree. The course duration is typically five and a half years, including a one-year internship. It equips students with essential knowledge and skills to diagnose and treat various health conditions.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Must have completed 10+2 with Physics, Chemistry, Biology, and English.
  • Minimum aggregate score of 50% (40% for reserved categories).
  • Qualified in the NEET (National Eligibility cum Entrance Test) examination.

Curriculum

The MBBS curriculum is divided into three phases:

  • Pre-Clinical: Anatomy, Biochemistry, and Physiology.
  • Para-Clinical: Pathology, Pharmacology, Microbiology, Forensic Medicine, and Community Medicine.
  • Clinical: Medicine, Surgery, Obstetrics and Gynaecology, Pediatrics, and other specialized fields.

Career Opportunities

Graduates of the MBBS program have a wide range of career opportunities, including:

  • General Practitioner (GP)
  • Specialist Doctor (after further specialization)
  • Medical Researcher
  • Hospital Administrator
  • Medical Educator
